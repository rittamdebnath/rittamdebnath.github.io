About the Project

GO is a bike renting app that allows you to pick up the bike from one place and drop it off anywhere. It helps commuters to quickly rent a bike, if they see one at a nearby place or otherwise they can use the maps and navigate themselves to the nearest bike in their locality.

The Team

Our project team consisted of three people (Chief Executive Officer, Chief Technology Officer) and myself. All their different perspectives on the product we were building made working incredibly useful. Learning about the industry would have proven much more difficult without their help.

Design Strategy

Understand the Problem

Competitor Analysis

How do existing bike rental products solve user pain points of finding a bike and renting it?. So, I conducted competitive analysis of 4 bike rental companies RentonGO, Wicked Ride, Royal Brothers, Onn Bikes and other travel apps.

Creating the Persona

As a first phase of the product design process, I quickly carried out several interviews with people about their daily commute experience, what apps do they use and what kind of pain points they find in their experience.

Rakesh

He is a 19 year old student want to travel in this own vehicle but due to lack of money he can’t buy one because he has to save money for his tution. So he could afford to go by public transport but wants a hassle free ride.

Uday

He is a 34 year old husband who works at a company as a software engineer. Although he has a car, he likes to travel by a 2 wheeler due to heavy traffic in the city in the morning hours.

Madhav

He is a work-o-holic who also likes to travel. He is saving money to buy her own vehicle in future but as of now He is using cabs for her daily commute. Due to heavy surge prices in cabs, he want a mode of commute where he can easily afford a vehicle and also don’t worry about maintenance and stuff.

Identifying the User Needs

As per the findings about the target audience, We started working on the user journey. Here the User journey was a key process because we had to consider every possibility of how and where the user might utilize the GO bikes. So we created a user journey to map their assumed experience.

With the help of the journey, we:

  • Were able to find the actual pain points about the user travel experience
  • Understood how they interact with other apps
  • Understood the idea that would help them drive to a more hassle free ride
  • Established the Functional requirements and gather some ideas on how we could solve some of the problems.

Challenges

How to educate user at any point while using the app?

Initial Thought

We thought of including regular walkthrough screens which would educate the user on how to access the app in order to use the bike to go from one place to another and how to drop the bike at a safe location so that it could be easily accessible to other users.

Solution

We then came up with the idea of including a simple info button on the homescreen it self, which would help them navigate to the walkthrough screens at any point of the journey. These walkthrough screens where using the slide through interaction method which helped the users to look at the instructions quickly and understand the usage of the app.

Safe Locations to park the vehicle

Initial Thought

Since we were a startup and had only few bike points, where you can pick and drop the bike. We need to find out a way by which user understood that we had only few bike drop points where you can park the bike. This will not only ensure safety of the vehicle but also less hassle for the Field agents to gather bikes at one place.

Solution

Due to the restrictions that we faced, We had to come up with the smart solution of letting the user know that they have to put the bikes at a given safe location otherwise you have to pay extra charges. So I included list of safe locations in the trip details screen itself before the End Trip button which will ensure that they have gone through the list before ending the trip.
